Tropical Rainforests

Tropical rainforests cover only 6% of Earth's surface but contain more than half of the world's plant and animal species. These forests are found near the equator and receive heavy rainfall throughout the year, creating a unique ecosystem that supports incredible biodiversity.

Wildlife Conservation

Wildlife conservation is crucial for maintaining ecological balance. Many species like tigers, elephants, and pandas are endangered due to habitat loss and poaching. Conservation efforts include creating protected areas, breeding programs, and raising public awareness about the importance of preserving natural habitats.
